üîç What is "Search Radius"? ‚ñ∂

From Michael Levin's research on planaria regeneration: Biological systems can "search" for solutions to novel problems‚Äîeven problems they've never encountered in their evolutionary history‚Äîif they have sufficient energy, time, and accessible options.

Search Radius (0-10 scale) measures how many alternative solutions your brain can explore. High capacity = wide search (can see many options). Low capacity = trapped (stuck in local minimum, can only repeat known patterns).

Real-World Example:

Radius 8 (high exploration): When facing a workplace conflict, you can consider multiple approaches‚Äîmediation, reframing, escalation, compromise. Creative solutions accessible.

Radius 2 (trapped): Same conflict, but you can only see two options: comply or quit. Your brain lacks the energy to explore alternatives. You're stuck in a binary choice pattern.

‚ö° What is "Manual vs. Automatic Mode"? ‚ñ∂

Your brain operates in two computational modes, determined automatically by available energy:

Manual Mode (High Complexity): Ethical reasoning, long-term planning, creative problem-solving, multi-variable analysis. Requires ~15 Watts of power and high C_PFC. Enables moral agency and institutional coordination.

Automatic Mode (Low Complexity): Reflexes, habits, heuristics, tribal responses. Requires ~5 Watts. Fast and energy-efficient but cannot handle novel ethical dilemmas or complex coordination.

Landauer's Principle (Physics):

Erasing information (required for decision-making) dissipates energy as heat: E = kT ln(2). When stress raises your "system temperature" (L_GC), the energy cost per operation increases. If total cost exceeds your metabolic budget, the brain automatically switches to Automatic Mode to prevent thermal damage. This is thermodynamics, not choice.

The entire computational stack collapses from the middle. This is why chronic stress destroys moral agency‚Äîit's not just "stress," it's architectural failure of the cognitive system.
